Kabanata 22 titled "Liwanag sa Dilim," Jose Rizal vividly portrays a grand feast hosted by Captain Tiago, providing a platform for social critique and commentary. Through the opulence of the event and the interactions among the diverse guests, Rizal exposes the underlying hypocrisy, corruption, and societal decay prevalent in colonial Philippine society. This chapter serves not only to showcase the dynamics between characters but also to foreshadow future conflicts and unravel deeper truths about the society depicted in the novel.

As part of our NSTP (National Service Training Program), my classmates and I participated in a community service project focused on cleaning the environment. We spent a day picking up litter, sorting recyclables, and educating local residents about the importance of maintaining a clean and sustainable environment. I documented the entire experience in my vlog, capturing the teamwork, the impact we made, and the lessons we learned about environmental stewardship. It was a rewarding experience that highlighted the power of collective effort in making a positive difference in our community.
